The term “financial charge” refers to the same thing as “interest?

A finance charge is any fee that represents the cost of credit, or the cost of borrowing, under the laws of the United States. The difference between financing charges and interest in personal finance is that finance charges are just the dollar amount paid to borrow money, but interest is a percentage amount paid, such as the annual percentage rate (APR).
