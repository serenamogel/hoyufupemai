Early Life and Career Beginnings

Mario Carbone was born and raised in Queens, New York. His passion for cooking was ignited at a young age, and he pursued this passion by attending the Culinary Institute of America. After graduation, he honed his skills in several prestigious kitchens, including the renowned Daniel Boulud’s restaurant, Daniel.

Establishing Major Food Group

In 2010, Carbone partnered with Rich Torrisi and Jeff Zalaznick to form Major Food Group. Their first venture, Torrisi Italian Specialties, was a hit, earning rave reviews and setting the stage for their future success.

Expansion and Success

Following the success of their first restaurant, the trio opened several other establishments, including Carbone, Parm, and Dirty French. Each restaurant has its unique style and menu, reflecting the diverse culinary skills and creativity of the team.

Carbone’s Culinary Style

Carbone’s cooking style is a blend of traditional Italian-American cuisine with a modern twist. He is known for his attention to detail, innovative use of ingredients, and ability to create a memorable dining experience.

Signature Dishes

Some of Carbone’s signature dishes include Spicy Rigatoni Vodka, Veal Parmesan, and Lobster Fra Diavolo. These dishes have become favorites among patrons, contributing significantly to the restaurants’ popularity and success.

Recognition and Awards

Carbone’s culinary skills and innovative approach have not gone unnoticed. He has received several awards and recognitions, including a Michelin star for his restaurant Carbone.

Impact on the Culinary Scene

Carbone’s influence extends beyond his restaurants. He has played a significant role in shaping the New York culinary scene, introducing new flavors and dining experiences to the city.
